Part 1: Self-Reflection

Looking back on all the modules, I can honestly say they helped me understand myself in a way I usually don’t stop to think about in everyday life. Before, I just saw myself as “me” without really breaking down what actually shapes who I am.

Through the Perspective of Self, I started noticing how my thoughts and feelings affect the way I see myself, especially during moments when I doubt or feel confident.

The lessons on Biological Influences and Body Image made me more aware that how I feel about myself is also connected to my physical health and how I view my body. I realized that my confidence can shift depending on how I take care of myself and how I respond to what I see around me.

In Personality and Identity Development, I reflected on how my personality isn’t something fixed; it changes and grows depending on my experiences and decisions.

Culture and Ethnicity helped me appreciate where I come from and how it quietly shapes the way I think and act, even when I don’t notice it.

Sexuality taught me the importance of respect both for myself and for other people’s identities and boundaries.

Mental Health and Wellbeing stood out to me because it made me realize that it’s okay not to be okay sometimes, and that taking care of my mental health really matters.

On this day, I see my current self as someone still figuring things out but slowly becoming more aware. I’m learning to understand myself better instead of just going through life on autopilot, and that alone already feels like real growth.

Part 2: How Others See Me

I interviewed two people for me to know on how others see me and how they would describe my personality and their impression of me. Interestingly, both had very similar impressions. They described me as someone who is naturally introverted and tends to keep to myself, especially when I’m in unfamiliar situations or around people I’m not very close with.

One thing they both pointed out is that I seem to have “strong walls,” meaning I don’t open up easily and usually take time before letting people get to know me.

They also mentioned that I can be a bit hard to approach at first. According to them, I don’t usually make the first move in conversations and tend to wait until I feel comfortable before engaging. However, they made it clear that this doesn’t mean I’m unfriendly.

In fact, they said that once I start opening up, I become much easier to talk to and more genuine in my interactions.

They also said that I can seem kind of distant sometimes, especially if someone doesn’t know me well yet. But they didn’t mean it in a bad way. For them, it just means I usually stay quiet and observe what’s going on first before I start talking.

When I heard that, I was like, yeah, that’s actually true. I don’t really open up right away, but once I get comfortable, I can connect better with people.

Part 3: My Manifestos

Personal Manifesto

I believe that life is meant to be lived with intention, not just by going through routines but by actively choosing growth in every experience. I value integrity in my actions, growth in my mindset, and resilience in facing challenges that shape who I am becoming.

I do not see failure as an end, but as part of becoming stronger and more aware of myself and the world around me. Every decision I make is an opportunity to become more grounded, more responsible, and more true to my values.

My Health Manifesto

I believe that true health is not only physical, but also mental, emotional, and spiritual balance. It is something that must be maintained daily through discipline, awareness, and respect for my own well-being.

I commit to caring for my body through proper rest, mindful habits, and healthy choices. At the same time, I will protect my mental space by allowing myself to rest, reflect, and recover when needed.

For me, health is not perfection; it is balance, consistency, and the willingness to take care of myself even in small, everyday actions.
